## 1. Data Collection Purpose & Lawful Basis

### 1.1 Primary Purpose
Personal data is collected **solely for appointment scheduling and direct healthcare delivery** at Mwein Medical Services. We operate under the lawful basis of:
- **Healthcare Delivery:** Fulfilling our obligations under Kenya's Health Act 2017
- **Explicit Consent:** Patient consent for processing health-related information
- **Legal Compliance:** Maintaining records required by medical and regulatory authorities

### 1.2 What Data We Collect

**Essential Data (Required):**
- Full Name
- Phone Number
- Service Type/Reason for Visit
- Preferred Appointment Date

**Secondary Data (Optional):**
- Email Address
- Brief Health Concern Description
- Additional Notes

**What We Do NOT Collect:**
- Detailed medical history (collected in-person)
- Insurance account numbers
- Payment card information
- Identification document numbers online
- Sensitive biometric data online

## 2. Data Protection Standards

### 2.1 Legal Compliance Framework
All data handling complies with:
- **Kenya Data Protection Act 2019** - Primary data protection regulation
- **Kenya Health Act 2017** - Medical records retention requirements
- **Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018** - Data security and cybercrime prevention
- **WHO Patient Safety Guidelines** - International healthcare standards

### 2.2 Confidentiality Assurance
✓ Patient information is treated as confidential  
✓ Data is encrypted in transit and at rest  
✓ Access is restricted to authorized healthcare staff only  
✓ No sharing with third parties without explicit patient consent  

### 2.3 Data Minimization Principle
We collect **only the minimum data necessary** for:
- Appointment scheduling and confirmation
- Healthcare service delivery
- Clinical assessment and treatment
- Medical records maintenance

### 3.1 Appointment Booking Data
- **Duration:** 1-3 months after appointment completion
- **Purpose:** Appointment confirmation and follow-up scheduling

### 3.2 Medical Records
- **Duration:** 5 years from date of last service
- **Legal Basis:** Kenya Health Act 2017 requirements
- **Exceptions:** Longer retention if treatment ongoing or legally required

### 3.3 Patient Consent Records
- **Duration:** 3 years minimum (per ODPP guidelines)
- **Purpose:** Proof of explicit consent for data processing

### 3.4 Data Deletion
Patients have the right to request deletion of personal data:
- Request must be submitted in writing to mweinmedical@gmail.com
- Non-medical records deleted within 30 days
- Medical records retained per legal minimum periods
- Confirmation of deletion provided within 14 days

## 4. Patient Rights Under ODPP

### 4.1 Right to Access
Patients can request a copy of their personal data:
- **Request Method:** Email mweinmedical@gmail.com or phone +254 707 711 888
- **Response Time:** Within 14 working days
- **Cost:** Free of charge

### 4.2 Right to Correction
Patients can update incorrect personal data:
- **How:** Phone/email/in-person request
- **Verification:** Identity confirmed before changes
- **Update Timeline:** Within 7 working days

### 4.3 Right to Deletion
Patients can request deletion (with exceptions for medical records):
- **Non-medical data:** Deleted within 30 days
- **Medical records:** Retained per legal requirements (5 years minimum)
- **Process:** Written request to clinic

### 4.4 Right to Data Portability
Patients can request their medical records in structured format:
- **Format:** PDF or electronic copy
- **Timeline:** Within 14 days
- **Cost:** Actual cost of provision (not exceeding Ksh 500)

### 4.5 Right to Withdraw Consent
Patients can withdraw consent at any time:
- **Effect:** Applies to future processing only
- **Method:** Written or verbal request
- **Impact:** May limit our ability to provide services

### 4.6 Right to Lodge a Complaint
If data rights are violated, patients can file a complaint with:
- **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)**
- **Address:** Longonot Road, Upper Hill, Nairobi
- **Phone:** +254 20 2714300
- **Email:** info@odpc.go.ke
- **Website:** www.odpc.go.ke

## 5. Data Sharing Policy

### 5.1 Who Has Access to Patient Data?
✓ **Authorized Clinical Staff:** Doctors, nurses, clinical officers delivering direct care  
✓ **Administrative Staff:** Appointment scheduling and records management only  
✗ **No Third-Party Sharing** without explicit written patient consent  
✗ **No Marketing or Commercial Use** of patient data  

### 5.2 Exceptions (Required Sharing)
Data may be shared without consent **only when**:
- Required by law enforcement (with legal warrant)
- Public health emergency (disease outbreak response per Ministry of Health)
- Court order or regulatory authority investigation
- Emergency medical treatment (life-threatening situations)

**Notice:** Patients will be informed of data disclosure except where legally prohibited

### 5.3 Government Health Systems
Information shared with authorized government systems:
- Kenya Health Information System (KHIS)
- District Health Information System (DHIS2)
- Public Health & Sanitation Inspectorate
- Only as required by Kenya Health Act 2017

## 8. Data Breach Response Plan

### 8.1 Breach Definition
Unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of patient personal or health data

### 8.2 Immediate Actions (Within 24 hours)
1. Identify scope and nature of breach
2. Contain/stop unauthorized access
3. Notify clinic management and data custodian
4. Assess risk to patient privacy

### 8.3 Notification Requirements
Patients will be notified of breaches affecting their rights within **14 days** unless:
- Low risk to privacy and rights
- Appropriate security measures were in place
- Authority specifically requests delay

**Notification Method:**
- Phone call preferred (for urgent breaches)
- Email and/or SMS for confirmation
- In-person notification if contact details unavailable

### 8.4 Regulatory Notification
Notification to Office of Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) if:
- Breach significantly affects patient rights/freedoms
- Requires more than immediate patient notification
- Affects more than 1,000 individuals

**Timeline:** Within 30 days of breach discovery
