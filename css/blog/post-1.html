Understanding High Blood Pressure

Educational content only. This information does not replace professional medical advice.

What is high blood pressure? High blood pressure (hypertension) occurs when the force of blood against the walls of the arteries stays consistently too high. Over time, untreated high blood pressure can damage the heart, kidneys, brain, and blood vessels.

Common causes and risk factors

  • Age and genetics
  • High salt intake and unhealthy diet
  • Physical inactivity and overweight
  • Excess alcohol use and tobacco use
  • Underlying medical conditions such as kidney disease

Signs and when to seek care

Many people with high blood pressure have no symptoms. Seek medical attention if you experience severe headache, chest pain, shortness of breath, or sudden weakness â€” these may indicate a hypertensive emergency.

Prevention and management

Healthy lifestyle measures can prevent or reduce high blood pressure: reduce salt intake, eat a balanced diet, be physically active, maintain a healthy weight, limit alcohol, and avoid tobacco. When prescribed, take blood pressure medications as directed and attend follow-up visits for monitoring.
