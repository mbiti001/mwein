When Should You Get an Ultrasound?

Educational content only. For personalized advice, consult a healthcare professional.

Common reasons for ultrasound imaging

  • Early pregnancy confirmation and dating
  • Routine antenatal scans to assess fetal growth and well-being
  • Abdominal pain or suspected abdominal pathology (gallstones, liver disease)
  • Pelvic pain or gynecologic assessment
  • Assessment of soft-tissue lumps or fluid collections

What to expect

Ultrasound is safe and non-invasive. A trained sonographer or clinician will apply gel to the skin and move a handheld probe to capture images. Exams usually take 15â€“45 minutes depending on the area being scanned.

Preparing for the scan

Some scans require a full bladder (e.g., early pregnancy or pelvic ultrasound); others require fasting. Follow the instructions given when you book your appointment or contact the clinic for guidance.
