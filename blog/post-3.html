Importance of Laboratory Tests

Educational content only. Laboratory results should be discussed with a qualified clinician.

Why tests are important

Laboratory tests help detect conditions early, confirm diagnoses, monitor treatment response, and guide clinical decisions. Common tests include malaria rapid tests, full blood counts, blood sugar tests, urine analysis, and infectious disease screening.

When to get tested

  • When symptoms suggest an infection or other illness
  • As part of routine health checks or chronic disease monitoring
  • Before procedures or when referred by a clinician

Understanding results

Test results need clinical interpretation. Abnormal values may require repeat testing, further investigations, or treatment changes. Always review results with a clinician.
